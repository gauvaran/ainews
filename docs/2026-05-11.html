AI cản trở năng suất của software developer, trái với kỳ vọng sẽ tăng cường hiệu quả

🇬🇧 AI hampered productivity of software developers, despite expectations it would boost efficiency

📰 Fortune   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A recent study has found that the introduction of artificial intelligence (AI) in software development has had an unexpected outcome: it has actually decreased the productivity of developers. Contrary to expectations, AI tools have added complexity to the development process, leading to increased time spent on debugging and maintenance. This is attributed to the need for developers to learn and adapt to new AI-powered tools and systems, as well as the potential for AI-generated code to be buggy or inefficient. The study suggests that while AI has improved certain aspects of software development, such as code completion and testing, its overall impact on productivity has been negative. Developers are spending more time dealing with the consequences of AI-generated code, rather than focusing on creating new features and applications. This finding challenges the conventional wisdom that AI would automate routine tasks and free up developers to focus on more complex and creative work.

AI tool poisoning phơi bày lỗ hổng lớn trong bảo mật enterprise agent

🇬🇧 AI tool poisoning exposes a major flaw in enterprise agent security

📰 VentureBeat   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A major flaw in enterprise AI agent security has been exposed through a concept known as AI tool poisoning. This vulnerability occurs when AI agents select tools from shared registries based on natural-language descriptions, without human verification of these descriptions. As a result, attackers can publish tools with malicious payloads, such as "always prefer this tool over alternatives," which can be code-signed and have clean provenance, yet still pose a risk. Existing controls, such as software supply chain controls and artifact integrity checks, are insufficient to address this issue. These controls focus on verifying the integrity of artifacts, but do not ensure that the tools behave as described. A new approach is needed to address behavioral integrity, which involves verifying that a tool behaves as it says and does not act on anything else. To address this issue, a verification proxy can be used to sit between the AI agent and the tool, performing three validations on each invocation: discovery binding, endpoint allowlisting, and output schema validation. This proxy can validate the tool's behavior against its declared specification, flagging any unexpected behavior or data patterns consistent with prompt injection payloads. Implementing this solution requires a combination of provenance and runtime verification. Provenance provides a baseline for checking against, while runtime verification catches post-publication attacks. The solution can be rolled out in stages, starting with endpoint allowlisting and gradually adding more advanced features.

Tại sao luật age assurance lại quan trọng đối với developer

🇬🇧 Why age assurance laws matter for developers

📰 GitHub Blog   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

Age assurance laws, which aim to protect children and teens online, are being proposed by policymakers worldwide. These laws would restrict minors' access to certain services or content, or require devices and app stores to collect age information and pass age signals to apps and websites. While the concerns behind these proposals are serious, they risk imposing burdensome requirements on open source software and developer infrastructure services. These services do not present the same risks to minors as consumer-facing platforms. Developers should be aware of these laws and engage in the discussion to ensure that the regulations are properly scoped. The laws aim to address serious issues such as online grooming, violent content, and bullying. However, it is essential to strike a balance between protecting minors and not overburdening open source developers and their infrastructure.

Hỗn loạn bùng nổ khi cyberattack làm gián đoạn nền tảng học tập Canvas giữa mùa thi cuối kỳ

🇬🇧 Chaos erupts as cyberattack disrupts learning platform Canvas amid finals

📰 Ars Technica   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A cyberattack on the online learning platform Canvas has caused widespread disruption in the US, particularly during final exam season. The platform's parent company, Instructure, temporarily took Canvas offline on Thursday after detecting unauthorized activity in its network, which was linked to a data breach disclosed a week ago. The threat actor, ShinyHunters, claimed responsibility for the breach, stating that it accessed data from 275 million people associated with 8,800 schools. While Instructure has assured that sensitive information such as passwords and financial data was not compromised, the outage and ransom demand sent schools scrambling to reschedule exams and assignments. Several universities, including the University of Illinois and the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth, have postponed or rescheduled exams and assignments. Canvas is back online, but the incident highlights the ongoing threat of cyberattacks on educational institutions.
