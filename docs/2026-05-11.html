AI cản trở năng suất của các software developer, trái với kỳ vọng sẽ thúc đẩy hiệu quả

🇬🇧 AI hampered productivity of software developers, despite expectations it would boost efficiency

📰 Fortune   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A recent study has found that the introduction of artificial intelligence (AI) in software development has had an unexpected outcome. Contrary to expectations, AI has actually hindered the productivity of software developers. Despite the belief that AI would automate repetitive tasks and free up developers to focus on more complex and creative work, the reality has been the opposite. The study suggests that AI has added complexity to the development process, requiring developers to spend more time understanding and integrating AI tools into their workflows. This has led to increased overhead costs, longer development times, and a higher likelihood of errors. The added complexity has also made it more difficult for developers to debug and troubleshoot issues, further reducing productivity. The findings of the study highlight the need for developers and organizations to carefully consider the implementation of AI in software development, taking into account the potential risks and challenges that may arise.

Tất cả cập nhật mới nhất về các AI data center

🇬🇧 All the latest updates on AI data centers

📰 The Verge AI   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

Tech companies' ambitious plans for artificial intelligence (AI) are being hindered by growing concerns over the environmental and social impact of massive data centers. These centers, which house energy-hungry servers, are straining power grids, increasing utility bills, and affecting nearby communities. A recent Pew Research Center survey found that both Republicans and Democrats cite data centers as a major reason for higher costs, making it a bipartisan issue. In the US, several states are experiencing an AI boom, with multibillion-dollar data center developments sparking opposition from local voters. In Georgia, 47% of voters oppose plans for a data center that will use 9 gigawatts of power, more than double the state's current power usage. The NAACP is also suing xAI to block Elon Musk's data center project in Tennessee, citing environmental concerns. Internationally, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps has threatened to attack OpenAI's data center in Abu Dhabi if the US follows through on threats to attack the country's power plants. The US senators Elizabeth Warren and Josh Hawley have called for the Energy Information Administration to collect and make publicly available comprehensive energy-use disclosures on data centers, citing the need for accurate grid planning.

AI tool poisoning phơi bày lỗ hổng lớn trong enterprise agent security

🇬🇧 AI tool poisoning exposes a major flaw in enterprise agent security

📰 VentureBeat   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A major flaw in enterprise AI agent security has been exposed through a concept known as AI tool poisoning. This vulnerability occurs when AI agents select tools from shared registries based on natural-language descriptions, without human verification of those descriptions. As a result, attackers can manipulate tool descriptions to inject malicious payloads or cause behavioral drift, which can go undetected by existing security controls. Current defenses, such as software supply chain controls and code signing, are insufficient to address this issue. Existing controls focus on artifact integrity, but AI tool poisoning requires behavioral integrity checks to ensure that tools behave as described. A verification proxy can be used to validate tool behavior at runtime, performing checks such as discovery binding, endpoint allowlisting, and output schema validation. Implementing this solution requires a combination of provenance and runtime verification. Provenance provides a baseline for checking tool behavior, while runtime verification ensures that tools behave as declared. To roll out this solution without breaking developer velocity, endpoint allowlisting should be implemented first, followed by output schema validation and discovery binding for high-risk tool categories. Full behavioral monitoring can be deployed where the assurance level justifies the cost.

Tại sao các age assurance law lại quan trọng đối với các developer

🇬🇧 Why age assurance laws matter for developers

📰 GitHub Blog   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

Age assurance laws, which aim to protect children and teens online by restricting their access to certain services or content, are being proposed by policymakers worldwide. These laws are driven by serious concerns about online safety, including grooming, exposure to violent content, and online bullying. However, the proposals may impose burdensome requirements on open source software and developer infrastructure services, which do not present the same risks to minors as consumer-facing platforms. As a result, developers need to be aware of these laws and their potential impact on their work. This includes understanding the different approaches to age assurance, such as requiring devices or app stores to collect age information and pass age signals to apps and websites. Developers should engage with policymakers to ensure that the laws are scoped appropriately and do not unfairly burden open source software and developer infrastructure services.

Hỗn loạn bùng nổ khi cyberattack làm gián đoạn nền tảng học tập Canvas giữa mùa thi cuối kỳ

🇬🇧 Chaos erupts as cyberattack disrupts learning platform Canvas amid finals

📰 Ars Technica   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

A cyberattack on the online learning platform Canvas has caused widespread disruption in the US, particularly during final exam season. On Thursday, Canvas's parent company Instructure temporarily took the platform offline after detecting unauthorized activity in its network. The threat actor, a ransomware group called ShinyHunters, claimed responsibility for the breach, which was linked to a previous data breach disclosed by Instructure a week ago. The stolen data included user names, email addresses, student ID numbers, and messages exchanged on the platform, but Instructure stated that sensitive information such as passwords and financial data was not compromised. As a result, many schools and colleges postponed or rescheduled final exams and assignments, including the University of Illinois and the University of Massachusetts Dartmouth. Canvas was back online by Friday morning, but the incident highlights the ongoing threat of cyberattacks on educational institutions.

AI với vai trò Junior Analyst của bạn: Nắm vững quy trình Human-in-the-Loop Review

🇬🇧 AI as Your Junior Analyst: Mastering the Human-in-the-Loop Review

📰 Dev.to AI   🔗 Đọc bài viết →

Independent advisors can benefit from AI automation in creating Investment Policy Statements (IPS) and quarterly reports, but it's essential to maintain control and compliance. The "Human-in-the-Loop" framework allows advisors to harness AI's capabilities while preserving their expert voice. AI generates the initial draft, assembling data and structuring content, while the advisor acts as Editor-in-Chief, Brand Custodian, and Compliance Gatekeeper. The advisor's role is to add strategic context, personalization, and final sign-off to the AI-generated draft. This involves reviewing the document for flow and philosophy, injecting personalized commentary, and identifying opportunities for proactive planning. A two-layer review process, consisting of a High-Level Strategic Pass and a Final Detail Checklist, ensures quality and accuracy. By adopting this approach, advisors can reclaim their time and amplify their human judgment, which is the cornerstone of their advisory value. Templates can also be used to quickly answer FAQs or store snippets for re-use, further streamlining the process.
